Getting there & around

Travel

Most of you are coming a long way — thank you for making the trip. The shape of it is simple: fly into Tokyo, then take the bullet train up to Karuizawa for the second celebration.

Flying into Tokyo Airports

  • Haneda (HND) is the closer airportAround 30–45 minutes into the city by train or taxi. Choose it over Narita if your flights give you the option.
  • Narita (NRT) works tooRoughly 60–90 minutes in. The Keisei Skyliner (to Ueno, ~41 min) or the Narita Express (to Tokyo/Shinjuku) are the easy routes.
  • US & UK passports: no visa neededVisa-free for tourist stays up to 90 days — just bring a valid passport. No JESTA or advance form is required for November 2026.

Tokyo → Karuizawa Bullet train

  • ~70 minutes, direct, no changesThe Hokuriku Shinkansen from Tokyo Station (also stops at Ueno). Take an Asama or Hakutaka service — the faster Kagayaki does not stop at Karuizawa.
  • Around ¥5,500–6,000 one wayReserved seats are worth booking ahead for mid-November. Covered by the Japan Rail Pass and the cheaper JR East Tokyo Wide Pass.
  • The venue is minutes from the stationA 5-minute taxi or 15-minute walk, or the hotel's free shuttle by reservation.

Grab an IC card (Suica or Pasmo) for tap-and-go on trains, buses and convenience stores. iPhone users can add a Welcome Suica in Apple Wallet and top it up with a US/UK card — the simplest option, no ticket machines needed.

Where to stay

Accommodation

You'll want a base in Tokyo for the first few days, and somewhere around Karuizawa for the second celebration. We won't try to pick a Tokyo hotel for you — there are countless great ones — but here's where we'd point first-timers, all well-connected and easy to get around from.

In Tokyo Neighborhoods

  • ShinjukuThe biggest hub — dining, shopping and nightlife at every budget. The full Tokyo experience, always buzzing.
  • ShibuyaCentral and lively, trendy cafés and shops, well-connected across the city.
  • Marunouchi / Tokyo StationPolished and supremely connected — and where the Karuizawa bullet train leaves from.
  • GinzaUpscale and elegant; refined shopping and dining, quiet in the evenings.
  • Roppongi / AkasakaClosest to the shrine — cosmopolitan, great restaurants and art, calmer around Akasaka.

In Karuizawa Our hotel picks

  • Kyukaruizawa Kikyo, Curio Collection by HiltonThe wedding venue itself — stay where it all happens, in Old Karuizawa. The loveliest, most effortless base for the day.
  • Le Grand Kyu KaruizawaA short walk from the venue in Old Karuizawa — a lovely, convenient spot right by the old main street.
  • Karuizawa Prince Hotel (West)Very comfortable, set by the station and the outlet plaza; an easy, well-run choice.
  • Hotel Indigo KaruizawaAnother very comfortable stay, near the station — and worth a look if you hold a premium travel or IHG card for the perks.
  • Holiday Inn Resort ANA Karuizawa by IHGGreat value if you're using an IHG credit (e.g. the Chase IHG card). Note it's well out of town — best saved for after the wedding.

While you're here

Things to do

Make a trip of it. Mid-November is late autumn — crisp air and city lights in Tokyo, where the leaves should be near their peak, and bracing mountain mornings up in Karuizawa.

In & around Tokyo

  • Senso-ji & AsakusaTokyo's oldest temple and its lantern-lined approach — the classic old-Tokyo morning.
  • Meiji Shrine & HarajukuA forest shrine beside the city's most exuberant shopping street.
  • Shibuya Crossing & Shibuya SkyThe famous scramble, best seen from the rooftop observation deck at dusk.
  • teamLab digital artImmersive, room-filling light installations — book tickets ahead.
  • Tsukiji Outer MarketGrazing heaven — fresh seafood, tamagoyaki, knives and snacks.
  • Shinjuku & the golden-hour viewsNeon after dark; sweeping city views from the free Metropolitan Government observatories.
  • Day trip: Hakone or KamakuraHot springs and Mt. Fuji views, or the great bronze Buddha by the sea — each an easy day out.

In Karuizawa

  • Kyu-Karuizawa GinzaThe old main street — cafés, bakeries and craft shops, a 5-minute walk from the venue.
  • Harunire TerraceStylish riverside cafés and shops tucked among the elm trees at Hoshino.
  • Kumoba PondA mirror-still pond ringed by a gentle walking loop — the town's most photogenic spot.
  • Shiraito FallsA wide, delicate fan of spring-fed "white thread" cascades in the forest.
  • Tombo-no-Yu onsenA well-loved hot spring near Harunire Terrace — perfect after a cold day out.
  • Prince Shopping PlazaA huge outlet mall right by the station, if the weather turns.

Beyond the wedding

Onward adventures

You've come all this way — you may as well keep exploring. These are all within easy reach of Karuizawa, mostly a short hop by train.

Kusatsu Onsen

~1 hr 20 by bus from Karuizawa

One of Japan's most famous hot-spring towns, built around a steaming "hot water field" in its centre. A wonderful overnight soak.

Nagano & Zenkō-ji

~30 min by bullet train

A grand 7th-century temple, one of the most important in Japan, at the heart of Nagano city.

Matsumoto Castle

~1.5–2 hrs via Nagano

The "Crow Castle" — a striking black-and-white original keep and National Treasure.

Snow Monkeys

~half day via Nagano

Wild macaques bathing in a steaming mountain hot spring at Jigokudani — unforgettable, if a bit of a journey.

Back to Tokyo

~70 min by bullet train

Round out the trip in the city — more food, more neighborhoods, more late nights.

The Nagano slopes

~2 hrs · Hakuba / Shiga Kōgen

Some of Japan's best skiing, though the season is only just opening in late November.

A note on gifts

Please — no gifts, and no money. Simply being there with us, after such a journey, is more than we could ask. Save it for your own adventures in Japan.

Packing note: Tokyo in mid-November is mild (around 10–17°C). Karuizawa sits up in the mountains and runs noticeably colder — comfortable by day, but chilly in the mornings and evenings. Bring a proper coat and warm layers for the second leg.
