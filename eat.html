Around Tokyo & Karuizawa

Where to eat & drink

A running list of our favourite spots for meals, coffee and drinks in both places — the ones we'd send our own friends to. We'll keep adding as the weekend gets closer.

In & around the city

Tokyo

Our marriage sponsor's bar · near the shrine

Bar After the Sunset

Just around the corner from Nogi-jinja in Akasaka, this is where we expect to land after the shrine ceremony on the 12th. Come raise a glass with us.

View on the map ↗

  • Kikunoi (菊乃井)Akasaka · Two-Michelin-star kaiseki — and where Ayumi works. A truly special meal, a short way from the shrine; book well ahead.
  • SuzunariYotsuya (Arakicho) · A refined Japanese restaurant we love.
  • ConcertoYoyogi-Uehara · Italian, right in our home neighborhood.
  • Tonkatsu TakeshinTomigaya · Excellent tonkatsu.
  • Sakutami (さくたみ)Great Japanese food with live jazz — a fun evening out.
  • BAR MALShinjuku · Another long-time friend's bar — go say hello.
  • Bar Old PalShibuya (Dōgenzaka) · A classic little cocktail bar.
  • The FamilyMart barShibuya · A proper bar hidden inside a FamilyMart convenience store — gloriously only-in-Tokyo.
  • Hanaoka StoreWest Tokyo · A personal favourite of ours — a real local gem, though a fair trek out from the centre.

Up in the mountains

Karuizawa

  • SawamuraOld Karuizawa · Karuizawa's beloved bakery & restaurant — superb bread and a lovely lunch.
  • GonseOld Karuizawa · A stylish izakaya on the old main street — seasonal Japanese cooking with a good sake & wine list.
  • La LuceNaka-Karuizawa · A lovely Italian restaurant built around local ingredients.
  • Gelateria GinaA wonderful gelato shop.
  • Piggies CafeKita-Karuizawa · A fun stop for good food if you venture up to Kita-Karuizawa.

The venue's own restaurant, SONORITÉ, is well regarded too — Shinshū ingredients with French technique, right at Kyukaruizawa Kikyo.
