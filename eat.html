Around Tokyo & Karuizawa

Where to eat & drink

A running list of our favourite spots for meals, coffee and drinks in both places, the ones we'd send our own friends to. We'll keep adding as the weekend gets closer.

In & around the city

Tokyo

Our marriage sponsor's bar · near the shrine

Bar After the Sunset

Just around the corner from Nogi-jinja in Akasaka, this is where we expect to land after the shrine ceremony on the 12th. Come raise a glass with us.

View on the map ↗

  • Kikunoi (菊乃井)Akasaka · Two-Michelin-star kaiseki, and where Ayumi works. The Tokyo home of Kyoto's century-old Kikunoi, chef Yoshihiro Murata's seasonal cooking. A special meal a short way from the shrine. Book well ahead.
  • SuzunariYotsuya (Arakicho) · A refined Japanese restaurant we love. Chef Akihiko Murata opened it in 2005 after years at the storied Nadaman, and it went on to earn Michelin stars. The steamed sea urchin custard is a signature. A small counter and a few tables, so book ahead.
  • ConcertoYoyogi-Uehara · Italian, right in our home neighborhood, and a longtime Michelin Bib Gourmand. The kitchen is built around the wine, mostly Italian and European, priced close to retail so you can be generous with a bottle.
  • Tonkatsu TakeshinTomigaya · Excellent tonkatsu, and one of Tabelog's hundred best for the category year after year. Try the SPF pork loin katsu, with free cabbage refills.
  • Sakutami (さくたみ)Great seasonal Japanese food and live jazz, a fun and relaxed evening out. Worth booking ahead.
  • BAR MALShinjuku · Another old friend's bar. Go say hello.
  • Bar Old PalShibuya (Dōgenzaka) · A snug, British-feeling cocktail bar named after the classic Old Pal, strong on whisky and whisky-based cocktails.
  • The FamilyMart barShibuya · A proper bar hidden inside a FamilyMart convenience store. Only in Tokyo.
  • Hanaoka StoreWest Tokyo · A personal favourite of ours, a proper local spot, though a fair trek out from the centre.

Up in the mountains

Karuizawa

  • SawamuraOld Karuizawa · A bakery and restaurant known for bread baked on site from dozens of flours. Good for breakfast or lunch, with free bread refills at midday.
  • GonseOld Karuizawa · A smart izakaya on the old main street, with a good sake and wine list and dishes like horse sashimi and chicken livers braised in red wine. You can bring the dog inside, too.
  • La LuceNaka-Karuizawa · An Italian kitchen built around Nagano and Karuizawa vegetables, with floor seating as well as regular tables.
  • Gelateria GinaA tiny gelato shop under 5 minutes' walk from the station, run by a couple. The owner trained in Italy and makes it fresh each morning, and it stays open late.
  • Piggies CafeKita-Karuizawa · A casual stop for burgers and pulled pork if you venture up to Kita-Karuizawa.
  • Cocktail Works KaruizawaOld Karuizawa · A cocktail bar and grill with a big craft gin list and plenty of fresh-fruit drinks.

The venue's own restaurant, SONORITÉ, is well regarded too. Chef Ryu Nakanishi cooks Shinshū ingredients with French technique, right at Kyukaruizawa Kikyo.
